    Now that you are mining, it is time to set up an exchange account. This will allow you to trade DOGE for BTC, LTC, or other coins for withdrawing/cashing out. Moolah.io is a popular exchange, as well as bter.com, and prelude.io. I use Gyft to buy gift cards to purchase stuff.

    Check the Active miners tab for your mining address; if you see that your dead hashrate is more than 50%, you probably need to adjust your miner settings. Setting the queue option to zero (0) can help, but usually it's lowering the intensity option that has the biggest impact on getting less or no dead shares. If you've already mined coins on a standard pool, you may notice an increased stale rate in your miner; 10% to 20% is normal and expected behaviour for P2Pool. The main idea is to keep your own stale rate lower than the P2Pool netowrk's stale rate (the pool rate). P2Pool payments can take up to 24 hours to ramp up to the maximum payout per block; this is due to the PPLNS payment system that nearly all pools use. This doesn't mean you won't receive payments within a few hours, just that it likely won't be equalized until about 24 hours later.

    Payouts require that you solve at least one share of difficulty greater than the P2Pool share target per day.
